The Dot-com Boom and Web 1.0 (Late 1990s)

Mid-1990s: The web experienced explosive growth. Companies and entrepreneurs recognized its commercial potential, leading to the "dot-com" era.

1994: Netscape Communications Corporation went public with its web browser, Netscape Navigator, and played a key role in popularizing the web.

1994: Amazon.com, one of the first major e-commerce websites, was founded.

1995: Windows 95 was released with the integrated Internet Explorer web browser, initiating the "browser wars" with Netscape.

1998: Google was founded, revolutionizing web search and becoming one of the most significant web companies.

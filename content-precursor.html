The Precursor to the Web (Pre-1990s)

1960s and 1970s: The foundations of the internet were laid during this period. The U.S. Department of Defense initiated ARPANET (Advanced Research Projects Agency Network), a research project to develop a decentralized communication network.

1980s: ARPANET evolved, and other networks like CSNET (Computer Science Network) and BITNET (Because It's Time Network) emerged. These networks allowed academic and research institutions to communicate and share resources.

1989: Tim Berners-Lee, a British computer scientist working at CERN (European Organization for Nuclear Research), proposed the concept of a "hypertext project" to facilitate information sharing among scientists.
